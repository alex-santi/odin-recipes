Chicken Flautas

Description

A classic Mexican recipe: Chicken flautas!
Tightly rolled tortillas filled with shredded meat, chooped veggies, and at times cheese. They are deep fried and served with sour cream, guacamole, or salsa picante.

Ingredients

  • Oil: 1 teaspoon vegetable oil and additional 2 cups for frying
  • Bell Pepper: 1/2 green bell pepper, chopped
  • Onion: 1/2 onion, finely diced
  • Chicken: 1 pound skinless, boneless chicken breast, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • Seasoning: 1 (1oz) package taco seasoning mix
  • Water: 3/4 cup water
  • Tortilla: 1 (10 ounce) package corn tortillas (such as Ortega)
  • Cheese: 1/2 cup shredded Cheddar cheese
  • Salsa: 1 cup of salsa (traditionally spicy)
  • Thoothpicks (24)

Steps

  1. Heat 1 teaspoon vegetable oil in a skillet over medium heat. Stir in the bell pepper and onion; cook and stir until the onion has softened and turned translucent, about 5 minutes.

  2. Increase heat to medium-high and stir in the chicken breast. Cook and stir until the chicken breast is no longer pink in the center, about 10 minutes.

  3. Shred the chicken using two forks, then stir in the taco seasoning and water. Let is simmer while stirring until the water evaporates.

  4. Stir in the Cheddar cheese, then remove from heat, and set aside.

  5. Spread salsa on your tortillas, then add aprox 2 tablespoons of your chicken in a line near the center of your tortilla. Tighly roll together and secure with one or two toothpicks along the ends. Repeat.

  6. Heat 2 cups vegetable oil in a large skillet to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).

  7. Fry the flautas in the preheated oil, no more than 4 at a time, until golden and crisp, aprox 4 minutes. Drain the flautas on a paper towel-lined plate. Discard toothpicks and top finished flautas with remaining salsa.

  8. Serve and enjoy!
